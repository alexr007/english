-------------------
Second conditional.
-------------------
It is using for imaginary, impossible, the low-probability situation in the present or the future.
Structure: if + (clause: past simple), would +(result: infinitive).
If he were smarter, he would win that game.
If you went to a meeting earlier, you wouldn't be late.
If I were you, I would use another approach.

-------------------
Second conditional, inverted:
-------------------
Structure: were + (clause: infinitive), would +(result: infinitive).
Were he been smarter, he would win that game.
Were you to wake up earlier; you wouldn't be late.
Were I you, I would use another approach.
